Arepa

Colombian Cuisine

Description

Arepa Colombiana is a traditional Colombian cornmeal cake, enjoyed across the country in various forms. Made from precooked corn flour, water, and salt, arepas are versatile and can be grilled, baked, or fried. They are often served with cheese, butter, or used as a base for other toppings.

Ingredients

  • Precooked corn flour (masarepa)
  • Water
  • Salt
  • Butter or oil (for cooking)
  • Chesse
  • Butter

Steps

  1. In a bowl, mix the precooked corn flour with salt.
  2. Gradually add water, kneading until a smooth, malleable dough forms.
  3. Let the dough rest for about 5 minutes.
  4. Divide the dough into small balls, then flatten them into discs about 1/2 inch thick.
  5. Heat a skillet or griddle over medium heat and lightly grease with butter or oil.
  6. Cook the arepas for about 5-7 minutes on each side, until golden brown and slightly crispy.
  7. Serve hot, optionally topped with cheese, butter, or your preferred toppings.
