Ajiaco

Colombian Cuisine

Description

Ajiaco is a traditional Colombian soup, especially popular in Bogot√°. It features chicken, three types of potatoes, corn, and the herb guasca. The soup is typically garnished with capers, cream, and served with avocado and rice. Ajiaco is known for its rich, diverse flavors, embodying Colombia's culinary heritage.

Ingredients

  • Chicken
  • Criolla potatoes
  • Sabanera potatoes
  • Pastusa potatoes
  • Corn on the cob
  • Guasca
  • Capers (Optional)
  • Avocado
  • Rice

Steps

  1. In a large pot, cook the chicken with water and salt until tender. Remove the chicken and set aside.
  2. Add the peeled and chopped potatoes to the pot, cooking until they begin to dissolve, thickening the soup.
  3. Add the corn on the cob, cut into pieces, and continue cooking until the corn is tender.
  4. Shred the cooked chicken and return it to the pot.
  5. Stir in the guasca and let the soup simmer for about 10 minutes.
  6. Serve hot, garnished with capers and a dollop of cream.
  7. Accompany the soup with sliced avocado and a side of rice.
